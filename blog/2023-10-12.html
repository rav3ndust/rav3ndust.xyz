bluesky domain verification

...or, more DNS shenanigans

2023-10-12

As you know if you have interacted with me or my @FOSSphones account over on Neighborli, my Fediverse instance, I like to play with and build upon new and open social media platforms and protocols we have coming around. Nowadays, we have ActivityPub, Nostr, and AT Protocol to interact with people over and build new applications on top of to empower everyone who wants to get away from the big tech platforms like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and the like.

Thankfully, with all of these new protocols and platforms springing up over the last few years and really starting to see some maturity, there is no shortage of alternative platforms you can look into and join, some even based on your interests (which is something we used to do back in the day when it came to joining a new forum dedicated to computers or another topic).

One of these new platforms is Bluesky, which was originally being developed as an initiative within Twitter as an exercise in building a new, decentralized social media protocol (that being the AT Protocol linked above). It is still currently in a sort of 'private beta', bringing people in through invite codes.

I got one awhile back and have been using the platform off and on a few times a week. I noticed you could verify yourself through your domain, and I checked out the process, which I thought I could share here if anyone finds the information helpful.

the process of domain verification

If you are loaded into the Bluesky web application, you will see the Settings icon. Click on that, and go down to Change handle that has a @ icon beside it. You will see some I have my own domain text that you can click. This will bring you a new snippet of text with some instructions. Do the following here:

  • Enter the domain you want to use where it says Enter the domain you want to use which is denoted by a @ symbol. For example, my entry here was rav3ndust.xyz.
  • It's time to work some DNS magic. Go to whatever platform you use to manage your DNS records (for example, I use Cloudflare, and be ready to create a new TXT record on the domain you entered above.
  • Once you're able to edit DNS records, add a new record. It will be of type TXT.
  • In Name, add _atproto in the text field.
  • You can set the TTL option to Auto.
  • In the Content field, you will want to have a look back at the text you have in your Bluesky settings. There should be a field showing you the Value that begins with did=. Copy this and paste it into the Content section of your DNS record.

When you're done with all of this, save your DNS record and wait a few seconds. Click Verify DNS Record on your Bluesky settings. If it worked successfully, you should now be able to see your domain as your handle on Bluesky!

If it didn't work right away, wait a few minutes and then try again. DNS changes can take a few minutes to resolve at times.

I always enjoy doing these kinds of integrations with my domain, and I've done a few more as well, including Nostr and Keybase. You should check out each of these open projects if you want to learn more about them. If you are on Bluesky, feel free to connect with me there!
